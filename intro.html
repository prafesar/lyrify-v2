✨ Grammar & Slang Highlights
Je ne regrette rien

Double negative with ne ... rien. Standard written French retains ne, whereas spoken French often drops it: "Je regrette rien."

No hay de qué

A very common Spanish idiomatic phrase used to respond to "Gracias". Literally means "there is nothing of what (to thank)". Equivalent to "Don't mention it".

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about Lyrify? Here is what you need to know.

How does Lyrify help me learn faster than traditional apps? ▼

Music triggers your emotional and rhythmic memory centers, which are distinct from analytical memorization circuits. By linking real-world slang, natural speech rhythms, and authentic melodies to context-aware translation annotations, you absorb vocabulary up to 3 times faster and retain details permanently.

What languages can I study on Lyrify? ▼

We support over 40 distinct languages! Key languages include Spanish, French, Italian, German, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, and Chinese, but our AI engine can process experimental rare languages, providing translation layers and parsing lyrics seamlessly.

Is Lyrify completely free to use? ▼

Yes, 100% free! Lyrify is guest-first by default. All of your saved flashcards, playlists, study sessions, and history are saved locally on your device via sandboxed browser SQLite databases, ensuring absolute data privacy and instantaneous local-first loading.

Can I analyze my own song lyrics? ▼

Absolutely! If you have a specific song that is not in our iTunes directory, you can paste its original lyrics in our Custom Lyrics Analyzer. Our AI will automatically identify the language, align the translation, and build the entire study workbench for you.
